# Roadmap: Clifford algebras, the Pin and Spin groups, and spin representations

The spin representations are the one part of the finite-dimensional representation theory of the classical
groups that the tensor-power engine cannot reach. Every irreducible representation of `GL‚āô` and `SL‚āô` is cut
out of a tensor power `V^{‚äód}` of the standard representation, and so is every irreducible of `Sp‚āô`; for the
orthogonal series this is **false**. The Lie algebras `ūĚĒįūĚĒ¨(2l+1)` (type `B‚āó`) and `ūĚĒįūĚĒ¨(2l)` (type `D‚āó`) each
carry fundamental representations, the **spin** and **half-spin** representations, whose highest weights are
`¬Ĺ(1,‚Ä¶,1)`, with **half-integer** coordinates. The weights of every tensor power of the standard (vector)
representation of `SO(V)` lie in the integral lattice generated by the vector weights `¬ĪeŠĶĘ`; the half-integer
sign vectors `¬Ĺ(¬Ī1,‚Ä¶,¬Ī1)` lie in the strictly larger weight lattice of the simply connected group `Spin(V)`,
so no spin weight is a constituent of any tensor power of the vector representation. These representations
exist because `SO(V)` is not simply
connected: they are honest representations of its **double cover** `Spin(V)`, and their carrier space is not
a tensor construction on `V` but the exterior algebra `‚čÄ¬∑W` of a maximal isotropic subspace `W ‚äā V`, made
into a module over the **Clifford algebra** `Cliff(V, Q)`. So
[the classical-groups roadmap](../ClassicalGroups/README.md) is genuinely incomplete without this one: it
builds the `B‚āó`/`D‚āó` irreducibles that are tensor constructions and must borrow the fundamental spin
representations from here.

Mathlib has a substantial Clifford-algebra library and, unusually, already has the Pin and Spin groups as
objects, but it stops exactly where the representation theory begins. It builds `CliffordAlgebra Q` with its
universal property, the `‚Ą§/2`-grading `evenOdd Q`, the even subalgebra, conjugation and reversal, the
module isomorphism `CliffordAlgebra Q ‚ČÉ‚āó[R] ExteriorAlgebra R M` in characteristic not two
(`CliffordAlgebra.equivExterior`), the base-change and product-of-forms decompositions, and explicit
identifications of small Clifford algebras with `‚Ąā`, `‚Ąć`, and the dual numbers. It defines `lipschitzGroup`,
`pinGroup`, and `spinGroup` as subgroups of the Clifford units and proves they are groups, that they act on
`M` by twisted conjugation keeping vectors as vectors (`spinGroup.conjAct_smul_range_őĻ`), and that they are
closed under `star`. What it does **not** have is anything that turns this into representation theory: **no
double-cover theorem** (nothing says `Spin(V) ‚Üí SO(V)` is surjective with kernel `{¬Ī1}`; only that the
conjugation action preserves `V`), **no spin module** (`‚čÄ¬∑W` is never made a Clifford module), **no
identification of `ūĚĒįūĚĒ¨(V)` with `‚čÄ¬≤V` inside the Clifford algebra**, **no spin or half-spin representation**,
**no highest-weight identification** of them as the `B‚āó`/`D‚āó` fundamentals, **no exceptional isomorphisms**,
**no Bott-periodic classification of the real Clifford algebras**, **no triality**, **no Lie-algebra
action through the Clifford algebra** (the quadratic realization of `ūĚĒįūĚĒ¨(V, Q)` exists for no form at all,
so no homomorphism `ūĚĒ§ ‚Üí ūĚĒįūĚĒ¨(V, Q)` makes a Clifford module a `ūĚĒ§`-module), and **no Kostant
`ŌĀ`-decomposition corollary** (the Clifford algebra of a semisimple Lie algebra with its Killing form,
under left multiplication by the quadratic lift of the adjoint representation, is isotypic for the
half-sum of positive roots). (The word "spin" does
not occur elsewhere in these roadmaps except inside "spine", and "Clifford" elsewhere always means
[Clifford theory over a normal subgroup](../InductionRestriction/README.md); the two are unrelated.)

This roadmap builds that theory, ending at the spin and half-spin representations as the fundamental
representations of types `B‚āó` and `D‚āó`, the low-dimensional exceptional isomorphisms, triality for
`Spin‚āą`, and Kostant's isotypy corollary for the Clifford algebra of a Lie algebra with an invariant
form. It rests on [the classical-groups roadmap](../ClassicalGroups/README.md) for the standard
representation and the orthogonal group, on [the highest-weight roadmap](../LieHighestWeight/README.md) for
the theorem of the highest weight and the fundamental weights (and, for Layer 9, its reductive `gl_n`
layer and decomposition toolkit), and on
[the root-systems roadmap](../RootSystems/README.md) for the `B‚āó`/`D‚āó` root data and the outer-automorphism
graph symmetry that underlies triality. Suggested home:
`TauCeti/LinearAlgebra/CliffordAlgebra/` (the structure theory, the double covers, and the real
classification, upstreamable to Mathlib on their own) and
`TauCeti/RepresentationTheory/Spin/` (the spin modules, the highest-weight identification, triality, and
the Kostant layer).

## Standing conventions

- **The base field is `‚Ąā` for the representation theory; real forms are named explicitly.** The spin
  modules, their irreducibility, and the highest-weight identification are stated over `‚Ąā` (algebraically
  closed, characteristic `0`), where a nondegenerate quadratic form has a maximal isotropic subspace of half
  the dimension and `Cliff(V, Q)` is a matrix algebra. The **structure theory** of the Clifford algebra, the
  grading, the filtration, and the Pin/Spin double covers are stated over a general commutative ring `R`
  with `[Invertible (2 : R)]` where that is the honest generality (Mathlib's `equivExterior` and the
  twisted-conjugation lemmas already carry `[Invertible (2 : R)]`), and specialized to a field where
  nondegeneracy and dimension counts are used. The **real Clifford algebras** `Cliff(p, q)` and the real
  groups `Spin(p, q)` are a separate, explicitly real layer; do not fold the signature `(p, q)` into the
  complex theory, where every nondegenerate form of a given rank is equivalent.
- **Reuse Mathlib's Clifford, quadratic-form, and Pin/Spin vocabulary.** The Clifford algebra is
  `CliffordAlgebra Q` for `Q : QuadraticForm R M` (`= QuadraticMap R M R`), with `CliffordAlgebra.őĻ Q`,
  `CliffordAlgebra.lift`, the grading `CliffordAlgebra.evenOdd Q : ZMod 2 ‚Üí Submodule R _` and its
  `GradedAlgebra` instance, the even subalgebra `CliffordAlgebra.even Q`, `CliffordAlgebra.involute`,
  `CliffordAlgebra.reverse`, and the module isomorphism `CliffordAlgebra.equivExterior`. The groups are
  `lipschitzGroup Q`, `pinGroup Q`, `spinGroup Q` with `pinGroup.toUnits`, `spinGroup.toUnits`, and the
  action lemmas `spinGroup.conjAct_smul_range_őĻ`, `spinGroup.involute_act_őĻ_mem_range_őĻ`. Isometries are
  `QuadraticForm.IsometryEquiv`; nondegeneracy is `QuadraticForm.Nondegenerate`; the exterior power is
  `exteriorPower R k M = ‚čÄ[R]^k M` and the exterior algebra `ExteriorAlgebra R M`. A representation is
  `Representation ‚Ąā G V`, bundled as `FDRep ‚Ąā G`. Never introduce a private synonym for any of these.
- **`pinGroup Q` and `spinGroup Q` are Mathlib's `Submonoid`s carrying a `Group` instance; the double cover
  targets an abstract orthogonal group.** Mathlib's `orthogonalGroup` and `specialOrthogonalGroup` are
  `Matrix.orthogonalGroup n R = Matrix.unitaryGroup n R` and its determinant-one subgroup, defined for the
  **standard** form on `n ‚Üí R`. The orthogonal group of an abstract `Q : QuadraticForm R M` is not in
  Mathlib; build it as `orthogonalGroup Q : Subgroup (M ‚ČÉ‚āó[R] M)`, the `Q`-preserving linear
  automorphisms, and `specialOrthogonalGroup Q` its determinant-one subgroup, and prove the identification
  with `Matrix.orthogonalGroup` under a basis diagonalizing `Q`. The twisted conjugation
  `x ‚Ü¶ (v ‚Ü¶ involute x ¬∑ őĻ v ¬∑ x‚ĀĽ¬Ļ)` is the map to `orthogonalGroup Q`. Turning the Mathlib content
  `spinGroup.involute_act_őĻ_mem_range_őĻ` (which lands in `range őĻ`) into a linear automorphism of `M`
  needs the linear equivalence `M ‚ČÉ‚āó[R] range (őĻ Q)`, an explicit named milestone: over a field with
  nondegenerate `Q` the map `őĻ Q` is injective, so `range őĻ ‚ČÖ M` and the twisted conjugation transports to
  a `Q`-preserving automorphism of `M`. Do not treat the conjugation action on `M` as immediate from the
  Mathlib range lemma.
- **Surjectivity of the double cover is stated over `‚Ąā`; the general field carries a spinor-norm caveat.**
  Over an algebraically closed field (`‚Ąā` here) `spinToSpecialOrthogonal Q` is surjective with kernel
  `{¬Ī1}`. Over a general field, `SO(Q)` is not the pointwise image of `Spin(Q)`: the image is the kernel of
  the **spinor norm** `SO(Q) ‚Üí K*/(K*)¬≤`, and rational-point surjectivity fails in general even though the
  map of algebraic groups is surjective. State the general-field version, if at all, in terms of the spinor
  norm; state the real forms `Spin(p, q)` with their component qualifications separately in the real layer.
- **The spin module is `‚čÄ¬∑W` for a maximal isotropic `W`, made a Clifford module by pinning the
  contraction coefficient to Mathlib's `polar`.** Over `‚Ąā`, a nondegenerate `Q` on a `2l`- or
  `(2l+1)`-dimensional `V` admits a maximal isotropic subspace `W` with `finrank ‚Ąā W = l`; the spin module is
  `S = ExteriorAlgebra ‚Ąā W = ‚čÄ¬∑W`. The Clifford operator `c(v)` acts by exterior multiplication `w ‚ąß -` for
  `w ‚ąą W` and by contraction `contractLeft` against the functional `QuadraticMap.polar Q w' ‚ąė (W ‚Ü™ V)` for
  `w' ‚ąą W'`. The defining requirement is the operator identity
  `c(x) ‚ąė c(y) + c(y) ‚ąė c(x) = polar Q x y ‚ÄĘ 1`, from which `c(v) ‚ąė c(v) = Q v ‚ÄĘ 1` (the Clifford relation)
  follows because `polar Q v v = 2 ‚ÄĘ Q v`. Do not write the coefficient as a prose "twice"; state it as
  `QuadraticMap.polar` and prove the anticommutator identity. Pin `W` and the resulting algebra homomorphism
  `spinAction : CliffordAlgebra Q ‚Üí‚āź[‚Ąā] Module.End ‚Ąā S` as named objects. The spin representation of
  `spinGroup Q` is the restriction of this Clifford action along `spinGroup.toUnits`; keep the Clifford-module
  structure and the group representation separate, related by an explicit lemma.
- **In odd dimension the single module `‚čÄ¬∑W` is not faithful for the full Clifford algebra.** For
  `dim V = 2l+1` the Clifford algebra is `M_{2^l}(‚Ąā) √ó M_{2^l}(‚Ąā)`, of dimension `2 ¬∑ (2^l)¬≤`, while
  `dim ‚čÄ¬∑W = 2^l`, so `spinAction` cannot be injective on all of `Cliff(V, Q)`: it factors through one of
  the two central-idempotent summands. Fix the action of the extra anisotropic vector `e` (with `V = W ‚äē W' ‚äē
  ‚ü®e‚ü©`) as the parity operator scaled so that `c(e) ‚ąė c(e) = Q e ‚ÄĘ 1`, which pins the summand. The faithful
  object is the direct sum of the two inequivalent spinor modules, not a single `S`.
- **The highest-weight identification is a Lie-algebra statement, against the abstract fundamental weights.**
  The highest-weight theory is stated first at the **Lie-algebra** level: `S` is a `LieModule ‚Ąā ūĚĒįūĚĒ¨(V) S` (via
  the bivector realization of Layer 3), and its highest weight is `ŌČ‚āó = ¬Ĺ(1,‚Ä¶,1)` (type `B‚āó`); the two
  half-spin summands are `ūĚĒįūĚĒ¨(V)`-submodules with highest weights `ŌČ‚āó‚āč‚āĀ, ŌČ‚āó` (type `D‚āó`). Mathlib's
  `Representation ‚Ąā G V` is a *group* object, so the passage from the `spinGroup Q` representation to this Lie
  module (the differential of the double cover, Layer 3) is a separate named lemma, not folded into the
  highest-weight theorem; the group-level `FDRep` statement is stated only where the differentiation
  infrastructure exists. The weights `¬Ĺ(¬Ī1,‚Ä¶,¬Ī1)` live in the weight lattice of
  [the root-systems roadmap](../RootSystems/README.md), strictly larger than the lattice generated by the
  vector weights `¬ĪeŠĶĘ`, which is why they are not constituents of tensor powers of the vector representation.
  State the identification against the fundamental weights of
  [the highest-weight roadmap](../LieHighestWeight/README.md) and the `B‚āó`/`D‚āó` root data, not against an ad
  hoc coordinate vector; this requires the explicit choice of a split Cartan and Borel of `ūĚĒįūĚĒ¨(V)` and an
  identification of its roots with the abstract `B‚āó`/`D‚āó` root system (Layer 5). The Lie algebra `ūĚĒįūĚĒ¨(V)` is
  realized here as `‚čÄ¬≤V ‚äā even Cliff(V, Q)` under the commutator bracket, and identified with Mathlib's
  `LieAlgebra.Orthogonal.so`; the spin action of the group differentiates to this bracket action, and that
  compatibility is a named target.
- **The Clifford grading is `‚Ą§/2`; the filtration by degree is separate and its associated graded is the
  exterior algebra.** Mathlib's `evenOdd Q` is the `‚Ą§/2`-grading (a `GradedAlgebra`), and `equivExterior`
  is a *module* isomorphism, not an algebra one. The **filtration** is `F‚āĖ = span of products of at most `k`
  generators `őĻ v`, including the scalars `F‚āÄ = range (algebraMap ‚Ąā _)` as the empty product; write it as
  `Submodule.span R {x | ‚ąÉ n ‚Č§ k, ‚ąÉ v : Fin n ‚Üí M, x = ‚ąŹ i, őĻ Q (v i)}`, not as a power of a submodule (powers
  of submodules in a noncommutative algebra are not the intended object). Prove multiplicativity
  `FŠĶĘ ¬∑ F‚Īľ ‚Č§ FŠĶĘ‚āä‚Īľ` and union `‚ä§` (`iSup_őĻ_range_eq_top`) first. The **associated-graded algebra
  isomorphism** `gr Cliff(V, Q) ‚ČÖ ExteriorAlgebra R M` is its own milestone: a *graded-algebra* isomorphism,
  proved either from the universal property and the filtration or by showing `equivExterior` respects the
  filtration and induces the exterior-degree quotient `F‚āĖ / F‚āĖ‚āč‚āĀ ‚ČÖ ‚čÄŠĶŹV`. This is a PBW-type theorem for the
  Clifford algebra, not an immediate read-off from the module isomorphism `equivExterior`. Keep the two
  gradings distinct in name and statement.
- **`ūĚĒįūĚĒ¨(V, Q)` for an abstract form is `skewAdjointLieSubalgebra (QuadraticMap.polarBilin Q)`, and
  quadratic elements are half-normalized commutators (Layer 9).** The orthogonal Lie algebra of an
  abstract nondegenerate `Q` is Mathlib's Lie subalgebra of skew-adjoint endomorphisms of the polar
  form; never introduce a private synonym. The quadratic element attached to a pair `(a, b)` is
  `‚Öü2 ‚ÄĘ ‚ĀÖőĻ Q a, őĻ Q b‚ĀÜ`, and its defining property is the bracket identity
  `‚ĀÖő≤(a, b), őĻ Q x‚ĀÜ = őĻ Q (polar Q b x ‚ÄĘ a - polar Q a x ‚ÄĘ b)`, the same formula that pins
  `soEquivBivector_wedge_mulVec` in Layer 3; do not hard-code the `‚Öü2` anywhere else. **Normal-ordered
  quadratics** (products `őĻ a ¬∑ őĻ b`, as in `F_ij = ¬Ĺ ő£_k d_ik d_kj`) differ from the antisymmetrized
  ones by a scalar, the **normal-ordering constant**; for a reductive `ūĚĒ§` this constant shifts the
  central component of the highest weight, so every statement names which of the two embeddings it
  uses. And **two `ūĚĒ§`-actions on `Cliff(ūĚĒ§, B)` must never be conflated**: the commutator action
  `x ‚ÄĘ c = ‚ĀÖq(x), c‚ĀÜ` (the exterior extension of the adjoint representation, not isotypic) and the
  **left-regular** action `x ‚ÄĘ c = q(x) ¬∑ c` (the isotypic one, Kostant's); every isotypy statement
  says which action it is about, and the two carry different names in `Suggested.lean`.

## The build, in layers

### Layer 0: the Clifford algebra, its universal property, and the two gradings

- **The universal property, consumed and packaged.** Restate `CliffordAlgebra.lift` and `hom_ext` as the
  working interface, with the basic identities `őĻ_sq_scalar`, `őĻ_mul_őĻ_add_swap` (the polarization
  `őĻ a ¬∑ őĻ b + őĻ b ¬∑ őĻ a = polar Q a b`), and, for orthogonal vectors, the anticommutation
  `őĻ_mul_őĻ_comm_of_isOrtho`. Record the functor `CliffordAlgebra.map` and `equivOfIsometry`: an isometry of
  forms induces an algebra isomorphism, so over `‚Ąā` the Clifford algebra depends only on the rank.
- **The `‚Ą§/2`-grading.** Consume `evenOdd Q`, the `GradedAlgebra` instance, `evenOdd_isCompl`, and the even
  subalgebra `even Q`; state `involute` as the grade involution (`involute` is `+1` on `evenOdd Q 0`, `-1`
  on `evenOdd Q 1`) and record `spinGroup.mem_even`, `spinGroup.involute_eq`. This grading is what
  distinguishes `pinGroup` from `spinGroup`.
- **The degree filtration.** `filtration Q k` is the span of products of at most `k` generators,
  `Submodule.span R {x | ‚ąÉ n ‚Č§ k, ‚ąÉ v : Fin n ‚Üí M, x = ‚ąŹ i, őĻ Q (v i)}`, an increasing filtration with
  `filtration Q 0 = range (algebraMap R _)` (the scalars, the empty product), `filtration Q 1` the scalars
  together with `range őĻ`, and union `‚ä§` (`iSup_őĻ_range_eq_top`). Prove multiplicativity
  `filtration Q i ¬∑ filtration Q j ‚Č§ filtration Q (i + j)` before anything downstream. This is not a power of
  a submodule.
- **The associated graded is the exterior algebra (a PBW-type theorem).** The graded-algebra isomorphism
  `gr Cliff(V, Q) ‚ČÖ ExteriorAlgebra R M` is a distinct milestone. Its graded pieces give
  `filtrationGradedEquiv`: `(filtration Q (k+1) ‚ßł filtration Q k) ‚ČÉ‚āó[R] ‚čÄ[R]^(k+1) M` in characteristic not
  two. This is proved either from the universal property and the filtration directly, or by proving that
  `CliffordAlgebra.equivExterior` carries `filtration Q k` onto `‚®Ā_{i ‚Č§ k} ‚čÄ‚ĀĪ M` and reading off the `k`-th
  quotient; `equivExterior` alone is only a module isomorphism, so the filtration-compatibility is content
  that must be proved, not assumed. In particular `finrank (CliffordAlgebra Q) = 2 ^ finrank M` for finite
  free `M`, matching `‚ąĎ‚āĖ (finrank M).choose k`.

### Layer 1: the structure theorem

Stated over a field `K`; the sharp form is over an algebraically closed field. **The proof runs in one
direction: the spin module of Layer 4 is constructed first, and the structure theorem is derived from it.**
The Fock model `S = ‚čÄ¬∑W` is built directly from a hyperbolic decomposition (Layer 4), the algebra
homomorphism `spinAction : Cliff(V, Q) ‚Üí End S` is proved surjective by exhibiting matrix units as products
of wedge and contraction operators, and then the equal dimensions `dim Cliff(V, Q) = 2^{2l} = (2^l)¬≤ = dim
End S` force it to be an isomorphism. Irreducibility of `S` (Layer 4) is then immediate, because `End S` is
the full matrix algebra. There is no reverse dependency: the structure theorem is not used to prove the spin
module simple.

- **Nondegeneracy and diagonalization.** Consume `QuadraticForm.Nondegenerate`; build the reduction of a
  nondegenerate `Q` over a field of characteristic not two to an orthogonal basis (a diagonal form), and
  over `‚Ąā` to the standard form `‚ąĎ xŠĶĘ¬≤`, so `Cliff(V, Q) ‚ČÖ Cliff(‚ąĎ xŠĶĘ¬≤)`. This is where `equivOfIsometry`
  does its work; it also fixes the hyperbolic decomposition `V = W ‚äē W'` used to build `S`.
- **The even-dimensional case.** For `Q` nondegenerate on a `2l`-dimensional `V` over an algebraically
  closed field, `CliffordAlgebra Q ‚ČÉ‚āź[K] Matrix (Fin (2^l)) (Fin (2^l)) K`, and the even subalgebra
  `even Q ‚ČÉ‚āź[K] Matrix (Fin (2^(l-1))) _ K √ó Matrix (Fin (2^(l-1))) _ K`. This isomorphism *is*
  `spinAction`, the Clifford action on the spin module `S = ‚čÄ¬∑W` of Layer 4, identified with a matrix algebra
  by the dimension count above; the even subalgebra's two factors are the actions on the half-spin summands
  `S‚Āļ, S‚ĀĽ`.
- **The odd-dimensional case.** For `Q` nondegenerate on a `(2l+1)`-dimensional `V`,
  `CliffordAlgebra Q ‚ČÉ‚āź[K] Matrix (Fin (2^l)) _ K √ó Matrix (Fin (2^l)) _ K`, while the even subalgebra is a
  single `Matrix (Fin (2^l)) _ K`. The center is `K` (even case) or `K √ó K` (odd case); the two
  central idempotents in the odd case are the source of the two inequivalent restrictions to `Pin`.
- **The seed of periodicity.** Consume `CliffordAlgebra.equivEven`
  (`Cliff(Q) ‚ČÖ even(Q ‚äē ‚ü®-1‚ü©)`) and `CliffordAlgebra.prodEquiv` (the graded tensor product over a
  direct sum of forms); these are the algebraic inputs the complex structure theorem and the real Bott
  periodicity of Layer 7 both rest on.

### Layer 2: the Pin and Spin groups and the double covers

- **The abstract orthogonal group.** `orthogonalGroup Q : Subgroup (M ‚ČÉ‚āó[R] M)`, the linear automorphisms
  `f` with `Q (f x) = Q x` for all `x` (equivalently `Nonempty (Q.IsometryEquiv Q)` realized as a bundled
  automorphism group), and `specialOrthogonalGroup Q` its determinant-one subgroup. Prove the isomorphism
  with `Matrix.orthogonalGroup (Fin n) K` under a basis diagonalizing `Q` (for the standard form), so the
  abstract and matrix presentations agree.
- **Vectors from the Clifford algebra.** Before the conjugation is an automorphism of `M`, one needs the
  linear equivalence `őĻRangeEquiv Q : M ‚ČÉ‚āó[R] range (őĻ Q)`, which over a field with nondegenerate `Q` comes
  from injectivity of `őĻ Q`. This is a named milestone, not a phrase; the conjugation action on `M` is defined
  by transporting `spinGroup.involute_act_őĻ_mem_range_őĻ` through `őĻRangeEquiv`.
- **The twisted-conjugation homomorphism.** `pinToOrthogonal Q : pinGroup Q ‚Üí* orthogonalGroup Q`, sending
  `x` to the automorphism `v ‚Ü¶ őĻRangeEquiv‚ĀĽ¬Ļ (involute x ¬∑ őĻ v ¬∑ x‚ĀĽ¬Ļ)` of `M` (well-defined by
  `spinGroup.involute_act_őĻ_mem_range_őĻ`, and `Q`-preserving because `őĻ v ¬∑ őĻ v = Q v` is central). Its
  restriction `spinToSpecialOrthogonal Q : spinGroup Q ‚Üí* specialOrthogonalGroup Q` lands in the
  determinant-one subgroup (the spin group is generated by even products of unit vectors, each contributing
  two reflections). Prove functoriality and that a unit vector `őĻ v` (with `Q v` a unit) maps to the
  **reflection** in `v‚ä•`.
- **The double cover (the summit of the layer), over `‚Ąā`.** For finite-dimensional `V` over an algebraically
  closed field (`‚Ąā` here) with nondegenerate `Q`, `pinToOrthogonal Q` and `spinToSpecialOrthogonal Q` are
  **surjective** (Cartan-Dieudonn√©: every isometry is a product of reflections, each realized by a unit
  vector), with **kernel `{¬Ī1}`** of order two (`MonoidHom.ker (spinToSpecialOrthogonal Q)` generated by `-1`,
  `Nat.card = 2`). Assemble the short exact sequences `1 ‚Üí ‚Ą§/2 ‚Üí Pin(V) ‚Üí O(V) ‚Üí 1` and
  `1 ‚Üí ‚Ą§/2 ‚Üí Spin(V) ‚Üí SO(V) ‚Üí 1`. Over a **general field**, pointwise surjectivity onto `SO(Q)` fails: the
  image is the kernel of the **spinor norm** `SO(Q) ‚Üí K*/(K*)¬≤`, so state the general-field result as that
  spinor-norm exact sequence, and the algebraically closed and real cases separately. This is what makes the
  spin representations representations of a cover rather than of `SO(V)` itself.
- **Simple-connectivity is a separate topological statement, not a prerequisite here.** The claim that
  `Spin(V)` is *the* universal cover of `SO(V)` is topological and is split by case, out of the main
  dependency chain of the representation theory:
    - **compact real case:** `Spin(n) ‚Üí SO(n)` is the universal covering for `n ‚Č• 3` (`Spin(n)` connected
      for `n ‚Č• 2`, simply connected for `n ‚Č• 3`), proved from path-connectedness of the sphere; this belongs
      with the real forms of Layer 7;
    - **complex algebraic case:** `Spin(V)` over `‚Ąā` is the **algebraically** simply connected group with root
      datum of type `B‚āó`/`D‚āó`, which is a statement about the algebraic fundamental group, distinct from
      topological simple-connectivity of the complex points;
    - **indefinite real case:** connectivity of `Spin(p, q)` for `p, q > 0` has its own subtleties and is
      deferred to a later topology roadmap.
  None of these is needed to construct the spin representations, which only use the double cover as an
  abstract group extension.

### Layer 3: the Lie algebra `ūĚĒįūĚĒ¨(V) ‚ČÖ ‚čÄ¬≤V` inside the Clifford algebra

- **Bivectors as a Lie subalgebra.** The image of `‚čÄ[R]^2 M` in `even Cliff(V, Q)` is closed under the
  commutator bracket; equip `‚čÄ¬≤V` with the induced Lie-algebra structure. `soEquivBivector`: this is a
  Lie-algebra isomorphism `‚čÄ[R]^2 M ‚ČÉ‚āó‚ĀÖR‚ĀÜ ūĚĒįūĚĒ¨(V)` onto the skew-adjoint endomorphisms, identified with
  `LieAlgebra.Orthogonal.so` under a basis. **The defining property is the action, not a hard-coded scalar:**
  the bivector `ő≤(u, v)` acts on a vector `x` by `ad(ő≤(u, v))(x) = B(v, x) ‚ÄĘ u - B(u, x) ‚ÄĘ v`, where
  `B = QuadraticMap.polar Q`. Choose the Clifford formula and scalar normalization (a multiple of
  `őĻ u ¬∑ őĻ v - őĻ v ¬∑ őĻ u`) so as to prove this identity against Mathlib's `polar` convention; do not fix the
  factor `¬Ĺ` before the convention is pinned. The bracket of a bivector with `őĻ v` is the infinitesimal
  rotation of `v`, which is the differential of the Layer-2 conjugation. Stated here for the standard form
  on `Fin n ‚Üí R`; Layer 9 restates the realization for an arbitrary nondegenerate `Q`, as
  `soEquivQuadratic` against Mathlib's `skewAdjointLieSubalgebra`, pinned to the same action formula,
  and builds the module-making machinery on it.
- **The differential of the double cover.** The Lie-algebra homomorphism differentiating
  `spinToSpecialOrthogonal` is the isomorphism `soEquivBivector` composed with the adjoint action; state the
  compatibility `d(conjugation) = ad(bivector)` as a named lemma, so the group double cover and the
  Lie-algebra identification are two views of one object. This is the hook that lets a `spinGroup`
  representation differentiate to an `ūĚĒįūĚĒ¨(V)`-module and be compared with
  [the highest-weight roadmap](../LieHighestWeight/README.md).

### Layer 4: the spin and half-spin representations

Stated over `‚Ąā`, for nondegenerate `Q`. This layer is constructed **before** Layer 1's matrix identification
is proved, and supplies it (see Layer 1).

- **A maximal isotropic subspace.** Over `‚Ąā`, a nondegenerate `Q` on a `2l`- or `(2l+1)`-dimensional `V` has
  an isotropic subspace `W` with `finrank ‚Ąā W = l` and (in even dimension) a complementary isotropic `W'`
  pairing with `W` by `Q`, so `V = W ‚äē W'` (even) or `V = W ‚äē W' ‚äē ‚ü®e‚ü©` (odd). Build `W`, the pairing
  `W' ‚ČÉ‚āó Module.Dual ‚Ąā W` induced by `QuadraticMap.polar Q`, and the isotropy `Q.IsOrtho` witnesses.
- **The Clifford module `S = ‚čÄ¬∑W`.** `spinAction Q W : CliffordAlgebra Q ‚Üí‚āź[‚Ąā] Module.End ‚Ąā (ExteriorAlgebra ‚Ąā W)`,
  the algebra homomorphism from the universal property, sending `őĻ w` (for `w ‚ąą W`) to exterior
  multiplication `w ‚ąß -` and `őĻ w'` (for `w' ‚ąą W'`) to the contraction `contractLeft` by the functional
  `QuadraticMap.polar Q w'` restricted to `W`. The defining check is the anticommutator identity
  `c(x) ‚ąė c(y) + c(y) ‚ąė c(x) = polar Q x y ‚ÄĘ 1` on `V = W ‚äē W'` (`w ‚ąß (w ‚ąß -) = 0` gives isotropy of `W`, and
  the wedge/contraction anticommutator on mixed pairs gives the pairing), from which the Clifford relation
  `őĻ v ¬∑ őĻ v = Q v` follows via `polar Q v v = 2 ‚ÄĘ Q v`. Pin the coefficient to `polar`, not a prose "twice".
  This is the **spinor representation of the Clifford algebra**. In **even** dimension it is an isomorphism
  onto `End S` (`dim S = 2ň°`), proved by exhibiting matrix units from wedge and contraction operators and a
  dimension count; this is exactly the Layer-1 structure theorem. In **odd** dimension `spinAction` is *not*
  injective: `dim Cliff(V, Q) = 2 ¬∑ (2ň°)¬≤` exceeds `dim End S = (2ň°)¬≤`, so it factors through one of the two
  central-idempotent summands. Fix the action of the extra anisotropic vector `e` as the parity operator
  scaled so that `c(e) ‚ąė c(e) = Q e ‚ÄĘ 1`; the faithful Clifford module in odd dimension is the direct sum of
  the two inequivalent spinor modules, not the single `S`.
- **The spin representation of the group.** `spinRep Q W : Representation ‚Ąā (spinGroup Q) (ExteriorAlgebra ‚Ąā W)`,
  the restriction of `spinAction` along `spinGroup.toUnits`; likewise `pinRep` for `pinGroup`. State the
  compatibility that `spinRep` differentiates to the `ūĚĒįūĚĒ¨(V)`-module of Layer 3 on `S`.
- **The half-spin summands.** The `‚Ą§/2`-grading of the exterior algebra splits `S = S‚Āļ ‚äē S‚ĀĽ` (even and odd
  exterior degree); `spinPlus Q W`, `spinMinus Q W`, each of dimension `2^{l-1}` in even dimension, are
  `spinGroup`-subrepresentations (the spin group is even, so it preserves exterior parity). In **odd**
  dimension `S` is irreducible and does not split; in **even** dimension `S‚Āļ` and `S‚ĀĽ` are the two
  inequivalent half-spin representations.
- **Irreducibility.** `spinRep` is irreducible in odd dimension, and `spinPlus`, `spinMinus` are irreducible
  and inequivalent in even dimension. This is immediate once `spinAction` is known to be surjective onto the
  full matrix algebra (Layer 1, proved forward from this module): a module on which a full matrix algebra acts
  is simple, and the even subalgebra's two matrix factors act as the two simple modules `S‚Āļ, S‚ĀĽ`. No circular
  appeal to a separately proved structure theorem is made.

### Layer 5: the fundamental representations of `B‚āó` and `D‚āó`

Built on [the highest-weight roadmap](../LieHighestWeight/README.md) (the theorem of the highest weight, the
fundamental weights) and [the root-systems roadmap](../RootSystems/README.md) (the `B‚āó`/`D‚āó` root data).
**These statements are stated at the Lie-algebra level:** `S` is a `LieModule ‚Ąā ūĚĒįūĚĒ¨(V) S` via the bivector
realization of Layer 3, and the highest-weight theory identifies it as a `ūĚĒįūĚĒ¨(V)`-module. The passage to the
group representation `FDRep ‚Ąā (spinGroup Q)` is a separate compatibility (the differential of the double
cover, Layer 3), stated where the differentiation infrastructure exists, not folded into the highest-weight
theorem.

- **A split Cartan and Borel, matched to the abstract root datum.** Choose the Cartan subalgebra of `ūĚĒįūĚĒ¨(V)`
  spanned by the bivectors `ő≤(wŠĶĘ, wŠĶĘ')` of dual isotropic pairs, and a compatible Borel; identify its roots
  with the abstract `B‚āó`/`D‚āó` root system of [the root-systems roadmap](../RootSystems/README.md), and prove
  the fundamental weights computed here correspond to the abstract fundamental weights. Without this explicit
  identification the highest-weight statement is only informal; it is a named milestone.
- **Weights of the spin module.** Diagonalize the chosen Cartan on `S = ‚čÄ¬∑W`; the weight of a basis vector
  `w_{i‚āĀ} ‚ąß ‚čĮ ‚ąß w_{i‚āĖ}` is `¬Ĺ(¬Ī1, ‚Ä¶, ¬Ī1)` with the signs recording which `wŠĶĘ` occur. The weights of `S`
  are exactly the `2ň°` half-integer sign vectors; the **highest** is `ŌČ‚āó = ¬Ĺ(1,‚Ä¶,1)`.
- **Type `B‚āó`: `ūĚĒįūĚĒ¨(2l+1)`.** `S` (odd dimension) is the irreducible `ūĚĒįūĚĒ¨(2l+1)`-module with highest weight
  `ŌČ‚āó`, the **last fundamental weight**; state `S ‚ČÖ L(ŌČ‚āó)` as a `LieModule`, with `finrank = 2ň°`, and
  separately that `spinRep Q W` differentiates to it. This is the fundamental representation of `B‚āó` whose
  weights lie in the half-integral coset, outside the lattice generated by the vector weights `¬ĪeŠĶĘ`, so it is
  not a constituent of any tensor power (in particular any `‚čÄŠĶŹ V`) of the standard `ūĚĒįūĚĒ¨(2l+1)`-module; it
  completes [the classical-groups roadmap](../ClassicalGroups/README.md)'s `B‚āó` fundamentals.
- **Type `D‚āó`: `ūĚĒįūĚĒ¨(2l)`.** `S‚Āļ`, `S‚ĀĽ` (even dimension) are the irreducible `ūĚĒįūĚĒ¨(2l)`-modules with highest
  weights `ŌČ‚āó = ¬Ĺ(1,‚Ä¶,1)` and `ŌČ‚āó‚āč‚āĀ = ¬Ĺ(1,‚Ä¶,1,-1)`, the **two fork-node fundamental weights** of `D‚āó`;
  state `S‚Āļ ‚ČÖ L(ŌČ‚āó)`, `S‚ĀĽ ‚ČÖ L(ŌČ‚āó‚āč‚āĀ)` as `LieModule`s, each of dimension `2^{l-1}`. Their sum `S` is the
  restriction of the `B‚āó` spin module. The exchange `ŌČ‚āó ‚ÜĒ ŌČ‚āó‚āč‚āĀ` is the `D‚āó` diagram automorphism, the
  seed of triality at `l = 4`.
- **Characters and the Weyl construction, cross-referenced.** State the character of `S` as an element of the
  group algebra of the weight lattice, `‚ąĎ_{őĶŠĶĘ = ¬Ī1} e^{¬Ĺ ‚ąĎŠĶĘ őĶŠĶĘ eŠĶĘ}` (the Weyl character formula specialized);
  the familiar product form `‚ąŹŠĶĘ (tŠĶĘ^{¬Ĺ} + tŠĶĘ^{-¬Ĺ})` is its evaluation on a maximal torus, valid only after the
  spin maximal torus and its character lattice are defined (half-powers of adjoint-torus coordinates are not
  functions on the adjoint torus). Record that the *other* `B‚āó`/`D‚āó` fundamentals `‚čÄŠĶŹ V` are built in
  [the classical-groups roadmap](../ClassicalGroups/README.md); together they are a full set of fundamental

### Layer 6: the low-dimensional exceptional isomorphisms

Each is an isomorphism of the spin group with a small classical group. `spinGroup Q` is a subgroup of the
units of `even Cliff(V, Q)`, so an isomorphism onto a bundled matrix group is not read off from the
representation landing in that group; it needs three explicit milestones per case:
  - **identify the even Clifford algebra** with a matrix algebra (`even Cliff‚āÉ ‚ČÖ M‚āā(‚Ąā)`, `even Cliff‚āÜ ‚ČÖ
    M‚āĄ(‚Ąā)`, and so on), so `spinGroup Q` sits inside its units;
  - **identify the defining condition** of `spinGroup` (the reversal/Clifford-norm-one condition
    `reverse x ¬∑ x = 1`) with the classical group's condition on that matrix algebra: determinant one for
    `SL‚āô`, preservation of the reversal-induced symplectic form for `Sp‚āĄ`;
  - **prove the image is exactly** the classical group, both directions, not merely that `spinAction` lands
    in it.

- **`Spin‚āÉ ‚ČÖ SL‚āā`.** For `V = ‚Ąā¬≥` (type `B‚āĀ = A‚āĀ`), `even Cliff(V, Q) ‚ČÖ M‚āā(‚Ąā)` with `dim W = 1`, and the
  spinor-norm-one subgroup `spinGroup Q` is the determinant-one subgroup, giving `spinGroup Q ‚ČÉ* SL(2, ‚Ąā)`;
  the `2`-dimensional spin representation `S` is the standard representation of `SL‚āā`. Over `‚ĄĚ` this is
  `Spin(3) ‚ČÖ SU(2)`.
- **`Spin‚āĄ ‚ČÖ SL‚āā √ó SL‚āā`.** For `V = ‚Ąā‚Āī` (type `D‚āā = A‚āĀ √ó A‚āĀ`), `spinGroup Q ‚ČÉ* SL(2,‚Ąā) √ó SL(2,‚Ąā)`, with
  `S‚Āļ` and `S‚ĀĽ` the standard representations of the two factors; this is the reducibility of the `D‚āā` spin
  module into the two `A‚āĀ` standards.
- **`Spin‚āÖ ‚ČÖ Sp‚āĄ`.** For `V = ‚Ąā‚ĀĶ` (type `B‚āā = C‚āā`), `spinGroup Q ‚ČÉ* Sp(4, ‚Ąā)` (`Matrix.symplecticGroup`),
  with the `4`-dimensional spin representation `S` the standard representation of `Sp‚āĄ`; the symplectic form
  on `S` comes from the reversal antiautomorphism.
- **`Spin‚āÜ ‚ČÖ SL‚āĄ`.** For `V = ‚Ąā‚Ā∂` (type `D‚āÉ = A‚āÉ`), `spinGroup Q ‚ČÉ* SL(4, ‚Ąā)`, with `S‚Āļ ‚ČÖ ‚Ąā‚Āī` the standard
  representation and `S‚ĀĽ ‚ČÖ (‚Ąā‚Āī)*` its dual; `V ‚ČÖ ‚čÄ¬≤(‚Ąā‚Āī)`. These four exhaust the coincidences of the
  `B‚āó`/`D‚āó` diagrams with the `A‚āó`/`C‚āó` diagrams.

### Layer 7: real Clifford algebras, Bott periodicity, and `Spin(p, q)`

- **The real forms `Cliff(p, q)`.** `realCliffordForm p q : QuadraticForm ‚ĄĚ (Fin (p+q) ‚Üí ‚ĄĚ)`, the diagonal
  form with `p` entries `+1` and `q` entries `-1`, and `Cliff(p, q) = CliffordAlgebra (realCliffordForm p q)`.
  The sign convention is fixed by four base entries stated as **definitional acceptance tests**, so the
  indexing of the periodicity table is unambiguous:
  `Cliff(1,0) ‚ČÖ ‚ĄĚ √ó ‚ĄĚ`, `Cliff(0,1) ‚ČÖ ‚Ąā`, `Cliff(0,2) ‚ČÖ ‚Ąć`, `Cliff(1,1) ‚ČÖ M‚āā(‚ĄĚ)`.
  Here `Cliff(0,1) ‚ČÖ ‚Ąā` is Mathlib's `CliffordAlgebraComplex.equiv` (its `Q r = -(r ¬∑ r)` matches the `q`
  entry `-1`), and `Cliff(0,2) ‚ČÖ ‚Ąć` is `CliffordAlgebraQuaternion.equiv`.
- **Bott periodicity.** The isomorphisms `Cliff(p+1, q+1) ‚ČÖ Cliff(p, q) ‚äóŠĶ£ M‚āā(‚ĄĚ)`,
  `Cliff(p+8, q) ‚ČÖ Cliff(p, q) ‚äóŠĶ£ M‚āĀ‚āÜ(‚ĄĚ)` (mod-`8` periodicity), built from
  `CliffordAlgebra.equivEven` and `CliffordAlgebra.prodEquiv`; the resulting **classification table** of
  `Cliff(p, q)` as a matrix algebra over `‚ĄĚ`, `‚Ąā`, or `‚Ąć` (the eightfold way), stated as a function of
  `(q - p) mod 8` **in the convention fixed by the four base entries above** (references that make generators
  square to `+1` index by `(p - q) mod 8` instead; the base-entry tests pin which is meant). This is the real
  refinement of Layer 1's complex structure theorem, where all forms of a rank are equivalent.
- **The real spin groups.** `spinPQ p q := spinGroup (realCliffordForm p q)`, the double cover of
  `SO(p, q)` from Layer 2 applied to the real form; the compact `Spin(n) = spinPQ n 0` and the split and
  Lorentzian forms `Spin(p, q)`. The real double cover of `SO(p, q)` carries the component qualifications of
  the general-field statement.
- **Connectivity of the compact spin group.** For the compact `Spin(n)`, prove `Spin(n)` connected for
  `n ‚Č• 2` and simply connected for `n ‚Č• 3`, so `Spin(n) ‚Üí SO(n)` is the universal covering, from
  path-connectedness of the sphere in `‚ĄĚ‚ĀŅ`. The indefinite `Spin(p, q)` (both `p, q > 0`) has its own
  connectivity subtleties and is deferred to a later topology roadmap; it is not a prerequisite for the
  representation theory.

### Layer 8: triality for `Spin‚āą`

Triality sits well above the rest of the roadmap and is built in two stages, the first much cheaper than the
second.

- **Stage one: representation-level triality (the root datum).** The `D‚āĄ` Dynkin diagram has an
  **order-three** symmetry cyclically permuting its three outer nodes; consume the graph automorphism from
  [the root-systems roadmap](../RootSystems/README.md) and lift it to an order-three automorphism of the root
  datum of `ūĚĒįūĚĒ¨(8)` permuting the three fundamental weights `ŌČ‚āĀ` (the vector node) and `ŌČ‚āÉ, ŌČ‚āĄ` (the two
  spinor nodes). At this level triality is the statement that the three `8`-dimensional highest-weight modules
  `V ‚ČÖ S‚Āį`, `S‚Āļ`, `S‚ĀĽ` are cyclically permuted by the outer automorphism; this needs only Layer 5 and the
  diagram symmetry.
- **Stage two: triality as an outer automorphism of `Spin‚āą`.** Realizing the diagram automorphism as an
  actual group automorphism `trialityAut : spinGroup Q‚āą ‚ČÉ* spinGroup Q‚āą` (order three, `trialityAut ^ 3 = 1`,
  and not inner) requires lifting a root-datum automorphism to the simply connected group, an
  integration/classification theorem for simply connected semisimple groups that is not supplied by the
  diagram symmetry alone. Its induced action on representations cyclically permutes the three `8`-dimensional
  irreducibles `V ‚ČÖ S‚Āį`, `S‚Āļ`, `S‚ĀĽ` (all of dimension `8`, the numerical coincidence that makes triality
  possible). State the permutation as isomorphisms `spinRep ‚ąė trialityAut ‚ČÖ (the vector representation)`, and
  so on around the cycle. This stage depends on infrastructure beyond the rest of the roadmap and is the
  summit.
- **The consequences.** The order-`3` symmetry of the three `8`-dimensional representations, the
  `Spin‚āą`-invariant trilinear form `S‚Āį ‚äó S‚Āļ ‚äó S‚ĀĽ ‚Üí ‚Ąā` permuted by triality, and the relation to the
  octonions (the multiplication `ūĚēÜ ‚äó ūĚēÜ ‚Üí ūĚēÜ` as the triality form); state the trilinear form and its
  invariance as the concrete outcome, cross-referencing the octonion structure where it is available.

Layer 3 realizes `ūĚĒįūĚĒ¨(V)` inside the Clifford algebra of the standard form; this layer frees that

corollary for `Cliff(ūĚĒ§, B)` under **left multiplication** by the quadratic lift of the adjoint
representation. The two-actions convention of the standing conventions governs every statement

- **The abstract quadratic realization.** The quadratic element `ő≤(a, b) = ‚Öü2 ‚ÄĘ ‚ĀÖőĻ Q a, őĻ Q b‚ĀÜ`,

  `ūĚĒ§ ‚Üí‚āó‚ĀÖK‚ĀÜ End(S)` (`cliffordInducedRep`). The two actions on `Cliff(V, Q)` itself are named apart:
  the **commutator action** (`cliffordDerivationRep`, the exterior extension of the `V`-action under
  `equivExterior`, **not** isotypic) and the **left-regular action** (via `Algebra.lmul`, the

  `‚čÄūĚĒįūĚĒ©‚āā ‚ČÖ 1 ‚äē V(2) ‚äē V(2) ‚äē 1` under the commutator action, against the four copies of the

## Ordering

The **core summit** of this roadmap is the complex spin representation theory: the hyperbolic Clifford module
`‚čÄ¬∑W` (Layer 4), the `ūĚĒįūĚĒ¨(V)`-action through bivectors (Layer 3), irreducibility and the half-spin splitting
(Layer 4), and the highest weights for `B‚āó`/`D‚āó` (Layer 5). The real forms and Bott periodicity (Layer 7),
the low-dimensional isomorphisms (Layer 6), and triality (Layer 8) are dependent extensions, each large
enough to stand on its own; they are included here for coherence but a contributor can reach the core summit
without them.

Layer 0 (the algebra and the two gradings) is the algebraic foundation and comes first; the filtration and
the PBW-type associated graded are used throughout. The structure theorem of Layer 1 is proved **forward from
the spin module**: Layer 4 builds `S = ‚čÄ¬∑W` directly from a hyperbolic decomposition and proves `spinAction :
Cliff(V, Q) ‚Üí End S` an isomorphism in even dimension by generation and a dimension count; Layer 1 reads off
the matrix identification, and Layer 4's irreducibility follows because `End S` is a full matrix algebra.
There is no reverse dependency and no circular use of a separately proved structure theorem. Layer 2 (the
Pin/Spin double covers) needs Layer 0's grading, the vector equivalence `őĻRangeEquiv`, and Cartan-Dieudonn√©
diagonalization; it is the load-bearing theorem that Mathlib stops short of, and its surjectivity (over `‚Ąā`)
is the single hardest target. Layer 3 (`ūĚĒįūĚĒ¨(V) ‚ČÖ ‚čÄ¬≤V`) needs Layer 0 and gives the differential of Layer 2; it
is the interface to [the highest-weight roadmap](../LieHighestWeight/README.md). Layer 5 (the highest-weight
identification) needs Layers 3-4 and the highest-weight theory of
[the highest-weight roadmap](../LieHighestWeight/README.md) and
[the root-systems roadmap](../RootSystems/README.md); it is stated at the Lie-algebra level, makes "the
fundamental representation of `B‚āó`/`D‚āó`" precise, and completes
[the classical-groups roadmap](../ClassicalGroups/README.md). Layer 6 (the exceptional isomorphisms) needs
Layers 4-5 for the low-rank spin modules. Layer 7 (the real forms and Bott periodicity) needs Layer 1's
structure theory and Layer 2's double cover, specialized to `‚ĄĚ`, and is independent of Layers 4-6. Layer 8
(triality) needs Layer 5's half-spin identification and
[the root-systems roadmap](../RootSystems/README.md)'s `D‚āĄ` diagram automorphism, and its group-level stage a
further integration theorem; it is the summit. A contributor can complete Layers 0-3 (the structure theory
and the double cover) and the `Spin‚āÉ` and real-form examples well before the highest-weight identification of
Layers 5, 8 lands.

Layer 9 needs Layer 0's filtration vocabulary, restates Layer 3's realization for an abstract
nondegenerate form (as new parallel targets; Layer 3's standard-form statements are unchanged), and
consumes Layer 4's `spinAction` for its spin-module variant; it is independent of Layers 6, 7, and 8,
so a contributor can reach Kostant's isotypy corollary directly from the core summit. Its
highest-weight content follows the Layer 5 device: isotypicity in Mathlib's `LieModule` vocabulary,
the names `L(ŌĀ)` and `L(őĹ)` in prose against
[the highest-weight roadmap](../LieHighestWeight/README.md) (whose reductive `gl_n` layer and
decomposition toolkit are the matching development), and the weight itself pinned in Lean only for
`gl_N`, where the Cartan and Borel are concrete matrices. Within the layer, the abstract realization
and `cliffordInducedRep` come first, the rank/parity pins precede the multiplicity statements, and
the CAR instance is a self-contained claimable unit needing only the abstract realization.
